"Let me explain what a Registry Index Map is.

[SHOW GRAPHIC: RIM sheet example]

A title deed describes ONE parcel. Your name, the plot number, the size.
A Registry Index Map shows ALL parcels in an area — every plot, every
boundary, every subdivision, laid out as a master government record.

Here's why this matters.

A fraudster can copy a title deed. They can forge a stamp. They can
create a convincing document for a plot that either doesn't exist,
has already been sold to someone else, or has had its boundary changed.

They cannot forge the Registry Index Map. It lives in a government
office. It's updated by licensed surveyors. It's the ground truth
that Kenya's courts, banks, and lawyers use when everything else is
in dispute.

Now — here's the critical question. Does your title deed match the
Registry Index Map?

Because here's what happens. Someone sells you land. The title deed
looks perfect. But five years ago, the owner filed a mutation form —
that's the document that records a boundary change — and subdivided
the plot. The RIM was updated. But the old title deed is still floating
around, describing land that no longer has those boundaries.

The mutation form costs KES 300. Three hundred shillings. That's the
price of information that could invalidate a multi-million shilling
purchase."

That list shows the old LR number and the new parcel number for
